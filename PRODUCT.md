# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Crypto-native investor/operators who manage wallets, exchange accounts, perp venues, and manual positions from a self-hosted dashboard. They use it during portfolio checks, exposure reviews, sync troubleshooting, and account setup.

## Product Purpose

Show a full crypto portfolio across onchain, exchange, and custom sources with enough account, asset, group, and history context to support exposure-control decisions. Success means the user can see what they own, where it sits, when it last synced, and which positions need action without leaking credentials or cross-user data.

## Brand Personality

Operator-grade, direct, and compact. The interface should feel like a working ledger for a personal CIO workflow: dense enough for repeated use, clear enough for fast decisions, and restrained enough not to compete with the data.

## Anti-references

Do not turn the authenticated app into a marketing dashboard, glossy SaaS hero, portfolio-performance toy, or decorative crypto terminal. Avoid overbuilt component ceremony, visual effects that hide data, and UI patterns that make standard forms/tables feel unfamiliar.

## Design Principles

- Data first, decoration last.
- Make provenance visible: source, account, group, sync state, and credential state should be easy to inspect.
- Favor compact controls and predictable affordances over novelty.
- Treat security boundaries as product surface: sessions, credential handling, and cache scope need clear user feedback.
- Keep exposure control as the primary product direction when choosing dashboard hierarchy.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for text and controls. Do not rely on color alone for positive/negative deltas or source categories. Preserve keyboard access for forms, tabs, dialogs, and account workflows; keep motion short and state-driven.
